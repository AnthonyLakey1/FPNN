Good Sleep Can Increase Overall Happiness and Mood, Study Shows

Author

Anthony Lakey

Published

November 18, 2022

Close

It is common knowledge that sleeping well has a variety of positive affects on the body. However, a new study shows that a good night's sleep can increase ones overall mood.

Sleeping well can be one option to help combat negative moods such as depression, chronic stress, and anxiety. Granted for some, these negative disorders can make it difficult to sleep well, but if possible, may have a great affect.

Researchers at the Department of Neurology (Bern University) found that this positive affect occurs during REM sleep. 

The brain seems to favor the discrimination of safety versus danger, but blocks the over-reaction to emotion, in particular danger. This can only occur once a person is in REM.

“Our goal was to understand the underlying mechanism and the functions of such a surprising phenomenon,” said Professor Antoine Adamantidis, of the Swiss university.

The best way for researches to have full control over this study was to include mice. In the process, they exposed some to a sound that they associated with safety and others to a sound they associated with danger. They then recorded the brain activity of each mouse while they were awake and asleep to work out how emotional memories are transformed during REM sleep.

Together, researchers found that two mechanisms work together to help process emotions. When the brain is awake it focuses on danger much more than the feeling of safety, but during REM sleep, the cells in the brain completely block out these emotions.

Without the ability to discriminate between these emotions, humans are more likely to experience excessive fear, which can lead to anxiety disorders.

This newfound research is just the beginning to a better understanding of how humans process emotions during sleep. Providing scientists with new ideas on how to help those suffering from mental health problems such as Post Traumatic Stress Disorders (PTSD).
