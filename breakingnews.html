BREAKING: Michigan Passes Proposal 3!

Story:

On June 24th, 2022, the U.S Supreme Court overturned Roe v. Wade. This bill supported women’s rights to an abortion and other contraceptives. With this decision, the U.S supreme court leaves it up to individual states to make their own laws on this subject.

Roe v. Wade was first passed in 1973, making the right to an abortion legal in all 50 states in the U.S. This bill has been legal for 49 years until 2022. With a bill being overturn so many years later, many Americans were upset at the U.S government. 

The ultimate question: Pro Life? or Pro Choice? These two beliefs  have very different views on the rights to an abortion. Pro Life does NOT support the right to an abortion. Those who identify as pro life have the mindset that "all life is sacred" and women should be forced to have a baby. While Pro Choice does support women’s choice in whether they want an abortion or not. Often the mindset "it's their body, they can do what they wish." Basically, with the overturn of Roe v. Wade, all 50 states were forced to adopt a Pro Life mindset in the halthcare and legal system. 

Without government support, women who became pregnant would be forced to go full term with their pregnancy. No special exceptions were made for those who needed an abortion for life-threatening medical purposes, or even rape. As one can imagine, this would force many young teens to become parents to children they cannot support. Not only would this put more children in the adoption agency, but also teen deaths to rise.

Some teens attempted their own abortions, while others traveled out of the country to get an abortion. Many states made abortions a felony charge. One state has gone so far as to not allow one female back into the state.

This November, Michigan included "proposal 3" on the voting ballot. This would return the constitutional right to an abortion.

2,480,000 MI residents (56.7%) voted "YES" on the ballot to support proposal 3. While 1,897,417 (43.3%) voted "NO"
